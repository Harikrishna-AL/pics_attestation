Transfer Certificate Attestation

A transfer certificate is a formal letter given by the school expert to its students to determine that the students have completed his/her education in their institution. In most cases, transfer certificate attestation is a mandatory requirement in global fields. Be that as it may, the certificate holder should verify the report by doing transfer certificate attestation before using it worldwide. Transfer certificate attestation must be done by concerned authorities before using it in the destination country. As indicated by international law, all transfer Certificates issued from various countries should check through the attestation procedure. The procedure is world widely famous as Transfer certificate attestation. In almost all the countries, attestation is used to find the authenticity of the foreign certificates. In straightforward words, transfer certificate attestation is one sub-category of the international verification method. Transfer certificate attestation is categorized as a non-educational certificate attestation. Every procedure identified with the transfer certificate attestation is completely controlled and coordinated by the government authorities. By confirming an official seal on the document, your report will be eligible in the goal fields. Transfer certificate is additionally prevalent in some proportional terms like Conduct Certificate, TC, leaving certificate and so forth.

A transfer certificate contains details like:

  • Name of student.
  • Admission number.
  • Date of birth.
  • Date of leaving.
  • Subject / class studied.
  • Reason for leaving.
  • Board of examination.
  • Character and conduct of student.
  • Recognition from the principal.

    • Today, this will help the holder with showing his/her educational status in the objective nation. Your certificate will be accessible in the objective nation while it is affirmed with the verification seal or imprint. Several procedures are required for finishing up transfer certificate attestation.

    Procedures for Transfer Certificate Attestation:

  • Notary attestation.
  • MEA (Ministry of External Affairs) attestation.
  • Embassy attestation.
  • MOFA (Ministry of Foreign Affairs) attestation.

    • There can be different reasons for getting a transfer certificate attestation. Getting an abroad school admission is the central matter of acquiring this attestation. Even though it is a non-educational certificate attestation, it is required in most international education. Sometimes it is necessary for employment purposes also.

    Requirements for Transfer Certificate Attestation:

  • Original certificate.
  • Passport copy.

    • These two documents are necessary for transfer certificate attestation. One can't finish an attestation procedure without these prerequisites. Migrant students utilize this affirmation to increase the chance for international higher studies. Before you present the report to the concerned schools or colleges, the certificate must be verified. If it’s not undergone through the transfer certificate attestation, it will be rejected by the time of migration.
