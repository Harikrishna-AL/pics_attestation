Certificate Equivalency Services in UAE

In the United Arab Emirates (UAE), certificate equivalency is referred to as foreign educational qualification recognition which is the assessment of the foreign qualifications to identify their compatibility with domestic qualifications. This process is used to approve foreign qualification education attained by people seeking to have their education recognized in UAE. The process of getting a certificate equivalency in the UAE is a practical process involving documents preparation and attestation, submission, and evaluation by the Ministry of Education. It also guarantees that foreign qualifications are accredited and can fulfil the required standards for educational and career purposes within the UAE.

The purpose of Certificate equivalency in UAE is as follows:

The purpose of certificate equivalency in the UAE is to promote and maintain standard fairness when it comes to the recognition of foreign qualifications. In this way, the UAE aligns the acquired standards with the international ones, enabling the holders of the degrees or qualifications received in the foreign country, to be integrated into the UAE workforce.

Documents that are needed when applying for Certificate equivalency in UAE include:

  • The applicant shall submit his or her original certificates in addition to the transcripts as applicable.
  • Passport copy
  • UAE Visa copy
  • Declaration form
  • Power of Attorney (if required)

    • The documents which have been attested also depend on the individual's objectives:

  • To work in foreign country.
  • To relocate to a new country.
  • For Higher education abroad.
  • For dependent access, etc.

    • The certificate equivalency process in the UAE involves the following steps:

    Application: Through this, the foreign qualifications recognition allows the applicant to fill forms which should be forwarded to the appropriate body in the UAE. Assessment: Some of the processes include a recognition assessment that seeks to give an idea whether the overseas qualifications meet the standard as provided by the UAE’s regulatory bodies. This can involve the assessment of the academic records, curriculum, and the performance on the exams by the student. Equivalency Decision: Depending on the evaluation results, the concerned body make a decision of whether the foreign qualification can be considered as a domestic equivalent. This decision is informed by aspects such as the subject matter, the length of the course as well as the study level. Certification: If the foreign qualification is considered equivalent, the authority furnishes a certificate of equivalency specifying qualifications acquired. Employment: The equivalency certificate is handy when it comes to job hunting or status or even when one wishes to further his or her education in the UAE. It assists people in proving to employers and institutions of learning of their capabilities in the job market.

    Certificate equivalency in the UAE offers several advantages:

    Access to employment opportunities: It enables those people who have acquired their qualifications overseas to have an equal chance with other people from within the country. UAE in particular has a very diverse economy that provide different employment opportunities. It performs in different industries such as oil & gas, construction, accommodation & food service, banking & finance, stores & other commercial real estate and more.

    The government also encourages foreigners to seek employment in the country through use of incentives. In order to secure decent employment opportunities in the UAE, the persons can focus on improving upon their skills through certain professional development courses or certification programs which are applicable to certain sectors of the individual’s respective interests.

    Transferability: The equivalency certificate arises to assure the public that the foreign education achievements are recognized and transferable within the country. Enhanced career prospects: Equivalency certificates help open up the sector to the individual by allowing them to prove a point that they are qualified to do the job. Educational progression: The main reason for issuing equivalency certificates is to ease the process of admission of foreign-trained candidates to higher learning institutions in the UAE.

    Professional Licensing: Many professional organizations as well as professional regulatory organizations in various sectors such as health care, engineering and teaching demand equivalency certificates with a view to licensing professionals.

    Legal and Administrative Recognition: The equivalency certificate enables authorities to give an official recognition to foreign qualifications hence making the legal and administrative procedures less complicated.
    Golden Visa: Golden visa refers to a form of residency that is granted to investors along other individuals such as their families entitling them to live, work, or study within the UAE. It exists in the investor visa program in UAE and is an attempt at encouraging more foreign investment into the UAE economy. The eligible investors are issued with a renewable visa, and are not required to pay some taxes. They are also free to take their family with them to the country depending on the agreement between the two parties.

    Generally, the golden visa has certain key advantages for investors and their relatives. They get the chance to invest in the establishment of the company and share the benefits of UAE stable economy and higher quality of living. It provides opportunity to enroll their family members in best schools or universities, and avail the best of medical facilities possible from anywhere in the world, and secure environment for their loved ones.

    The process of certificate recognition in UAE is an essential procedure that has the responsibilities of accepting and recognizing certificates that have been acquired in other countries. Based on the analysis of the UAE’s policy and the response to the identified challenges, it is possible to conclude that the policy is beneficial for mobility, equal opportunities and personal and professional development of individuals when a formal equivalence to foreign qualifications is provided.
